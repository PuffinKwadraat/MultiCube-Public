    Intro

    Minecraft Texture Packs are basically a collection of files used to change the textures of in-game elements, from blocks and items to full-on mobs and actual in-game environments.


    Think of them as mods that are purely aesthetic. They’re created, distributed, and installed with the sole purpose of changing the game’s visuals.


    Say you’re tired of the hyper-pixelated or blocky look of Minecraft. Use a texture pack to turn those square edges into something more sleek and realistic looking. Or use a different pack to keep the style of graphics but make them sharper, crisper, more HD. Or you could throw all that completely out the window and install a texture pack that turns Minecraft into a soft, pastel wonderland.


    Note: some texture packs do not get updated anymore so not al texture packs are compatible with the newest versions of Minecraft.

    1. Improved Default


    “Improved Default 1.14 – V1.6” is considered a “vanilla plus pack” that simply enhances the game’s textures. This means that the default vanilla textures are intact; they just look a lot better.


    Besides a few updated mob textures and better resolution, there are no other instantly obvious changes to the appearance. The most noticeable difference would be that some blocks now seamlessly blend with each other. The grass and stone blocks in particular no longer look like separate squares, which makes the overall world more natural and easier on the eyes.


    If you love the vanilla textures, don’t want to do any drastic visual changes, or just want to upgrade your game’s graphics quickly and cleanly, we highly recommend you check out Improved Default.

    2. Realistic Sky


    Continuing on the theme of small, subtle, and/or gradual visual changes, we have to mention Realistic Sky here. This resource pack (another name for texture pack) by Fire Eagle is an absolute stunner. It lets you play Minecraft with a realistic and gorgeous sky backdrop.


    That’s not at all. The pack is programmed to synchronize with weather and time settings in the game. So depending on the time of day, you get treated with a sunrise, a sunset, a bright and clear noon backdrop or a beautiful night sky full of stars.


    If you – or your Minecraft-counterpart – are a nature lover, you’ll absolutely fall in love with this pack. You can even combine this texture with other texture packs to create a realistic or hyper-realistic gaming experience.

    3. Jolicraft


    If you’re looking for a visual change that’s just a touch more dramatic, we highly recommend Jolicraft, a “whimsical texture pack” that is by far one of the most creative and unique options out there.


    It was first released in 2011, but Jolicoeur, the author, has been incredibly dedicated to improving it and keeping it up-to-date. We’re not exaggerating when we say that this pack is an absolute treat for the eyes. It utterly transforms the Minecraft world as you know it, turning it from some straightforward survival game into a quirky, lo-fi world that’s cuter, calmer, and chiller.


    Wolves’ mouths are lined with tiny teeth. The sun is a constant swirl of odd cheer. You get moody custom clouds. There’s a tic-tac-toe cake.


    It’s just a straight-up whimsical wonderland that continues to delight hundreds of thousands of Minecraft players almost a decade after its release.

    4. Quadral


    Quadral is a bright, vibrant texture pack that doesn’t stray too far from the default Minecraft aesthetic, with simple, charming textures that give your world a visual refresh. It’s a little more soft and gentle looking than vanilla textures, and it’s 16×16 resolution like native Minecraft, so it’s a great replacement for your next survival mode adventure. It also has an add-on pack for the ‘Biomes O’Plenty’ Minecraft mod, so you can explore modded biomes in the same Quadral style.

    5. JohnSmith


    Arguably one of the best fantasy texture packs available, JohnSmith is an insanely customizable resource that turns Minecraft’s colorful landscape into a mildly muted medieval masterpiece. Who knew Minecraft could look so good in quiet colors and somber neutrals? (JohnSmith, apparently.)


    From imposing castles with sprawling courtyards to fantastically unnerving skeletons, this texture pack is as detailed as they come.


    This pack is best appreciated at night. Once in-game darkness falls, that’s when the change in the graphics truly shines (as ironic as that sounds). The dark cobblestones, grungy rocks, and deep worn wood structures of JohnSmith’s texture pack all look incredible in graphically generated shadows and indoor lights.


    Another reason this pack is so cool? Customization. You literally get to pick and choose which parts of the pack you like and which ones you’d rather do without.

    6. Faitful


    The Faithful texture pack – like Realistic Sky and Improved Default – simply adds a more polished, high- definition shine to Minecraft’s vanilla base game graphics. It’s marketed as the “most popular texture pack in Minecraft,” and the numbers it’s garnered sure seem to support that claim.


    Most of Faithful’s charm lies in the fact that it remains “faithful” (we’re sure you get it) to the original textures, doing little more than blending edges, smoothing out surfaces, and adding an overall refined sheen to the in-game experience. It changes the standard 16x resolution to 32x, which – as you can probably guess – does wonder for visuals.


    The newer version even goes all the way up to a whopping 64x. If you live for HD, definitely grab this texture pack.


    Note: the official site of Faitful only supports up to 32x, if you want higher resolutions visit different websites.

    7. Isabella


    Shiny, sheeny, and ultra-HD is all well and good, but sometimes you just want something warmer and more muted.


    We get it. Nothing wrong with wanting something just a touch more natural in a world made of blocks and pixels. Hence, the Isabella texture pack – a truly gentle graphical resource that takes subtle to an art form.


    Whereas Faithful focuses on edges and resolution, Isabella focuses on the colors, hues, tones, and saturations. Every element, from the wood blocks to the stone facades, looks warmer, quieter, and more faded.


    It’s all very soft, quiet, and melancholic – words we never thought we’d associate with Minecraft, but here we are.

    8. Misa's Realistic Texture Pack


    Want the realism of Isabella with none of the proverbial rain clouds hanging over everyone’s heads? Then Misa’s Realistic Texture Pack might be more your speed.


    It’s got the same sort of desaturated, understated feel to it with regards to the colors and hues. But the general vibe of it is less melancholy and more raw, real, and unfiltered. The ground may be muddier and the surfaces may be grubbier, but the clouds – in contrast – are much fluffier and the lights indoors are a lot brighter


    It’s perhaps the most realistic Minecraft can look, given the game’s unique design.

    9. Glimmar's Steampunk


    Glimmar’s Steampunk pack is another highly-downloaded resource that isn’t just for people who like steampunk. It introduces such a drastic change to Minecraft’s visuals, this texture pack makes it feel like an entirely different game.


    If Jolicraft was too whimsical for you and JohnSmith’s pack wasn’t somber enough, Glimmar’s pack might be just the graphical variation you’re looking for. Enjoy the whole dark-Victorian-meets-SciFi feel as you stumble upon steam-powered trains and old-school, beat-up, steam-filled factories.


    It’s so detailed, so incredibly immersive, and it makes for some truly exhilarating gameplay. It also adds some new textures for robot factory workers and mechanical horses.

    10. Dokucraft


    Dokucraft, a 32x texture pack made by doku, has established quite the community. For over eight years, hundreds of programmers and designers have contributed to keeping this resource alive and updated for all versions of Minecraft. It’s almost impressive just how coordinated this entire project is. And as far as we know, it’s still ongoing.


    You can definitely check out their official About page to know more, but here’s a quick summary. Dokucraft is a medieval/fantasy-themed texture pack that offers 32×32 resolution. There are three different types of Dokucraft packs: Dark, Light, and High.


    As you might expect, the names are very indicative of the visuals. Dokucraft Dark has a grittier, grayer, desaturated feel. Dokucraft Light is the brightest of the three, with highly saturated colors and defined contrasts.


    Dokucraft High is a good balance of the two; deeper emeralds and crystal blues complemented with muted, darker undertones.

    11. Stratum


    Stratum is probably the most realistic texture pack in Minecraft. This texture pack has unique features like 3D-effects and compatibility with shaders. Check out their official website to find out more, see some amazing screenshots like the one above and see the systemrequirements. Yes, this is a real Minecraft screenshot. Note that higher quality texture packs like Stratum 1024x or 2048x are for the highest end computers and require a lot of RAM and are meant to be used in cinematics and can't be run on older devices. Furthermore, such texture packs cost money. You can get the 128x testversion for free.
